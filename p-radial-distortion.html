Radial Distortion


Grounded, I sigh back into your smile.

On the tip of my tongue is dying,

and I know this. I’m kid

kidding to you, blustering and

sycophantic. Do you want that?

Relentless casting up of authority, casting

out rejectamenta, spun descent above grinning

hands. Fawning absolutions prevent a

narrow ascension, raising the light up

to the dark windowpane looking across the field.

Holding fast to crimping postures is it

in your power to know now.


Love is an excuse for this. Loving me

I guess. Reiterative not restorative, a return each time

rewritten as this is how we do it this is how we


Wandering around in my nights and swooning.

I suppose there’s no security in it but is there in any.

Fresh hopes hurled across my room,

cloistered and reverberating,

nothing new to the no now you.
